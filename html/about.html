About Me

I've been familiar with programming since my 5th grade Computer Class where we had to make some simple Python code.
This code was really simple, like adding up numbers or looping through code infinitely.
Around that time, I was very interested in the concept of Discord bots, more specifically, making them.
I looked up a Discord bot making tutorial on YouTube, and saw this guy's video.
It was very easy to get started, and he explained everything very clearly, making it easy to understand what he was doing.
I enjoyed making that bot and it was a great learning experience for me.
Unfortunately, after 6th grade, I quickly lost motivation for coding, to a reason that is actually unknown to me.
Although, fast-forward to somewhere around October 2020., I wanted to make bots again.
Since my last semester's curriculum didn't include programming, I had to brush up on my Python skills.
I felt good because I started to code again, because now I actually had something to do on the side, other than playing video-games.
The first bot I made was called "Reaper" and I really enjoyed making it beacuse it felt refreshing to do something different in a very long while.
While I was looking for somewhere to host my bot, I came across BlueFox, the same hosting service linked in my Home page.
I made great friends in their official Discord server, including the CEO of the service itself.
Unfortunately, Reaper didn't last very long, as there were so many bugs I'd be better off with scrapping the code and remaking the bot.
That remake never really happened because I was getting bored out by the project, though I still had something in mind.
After getting more JavaScript experience, I decided to create a small development team with my friends where we would actually make bots.
But, history repeated itself, and after one of my friend's "incidents", the team lasted for maybe a week or two before we stopped due to inactivity.
For some reason though, one of the members of the development team decided to reopen and start over recently, so we'll see how that goes.
From here on, I can only assume what happens next. I've met some great friends over the past 5-ish months that I've actually been doing programming.
I'm really thankful for what I've got, who I've got, and how I've gotten here, and I can't even imagine how this phase of my life would turn out if it weren't for them.
With that said, I wish you all the best, and I hope you too succeed in whatever side-project or hobby you have.
Cheers,
~ John.
